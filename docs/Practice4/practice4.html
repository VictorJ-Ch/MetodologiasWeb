Love, Simon

Everyone deserves a great love story, but 17-year-old Simon, it's a bit complicated. The gay teenager hasn't come out yet, and doesn't know the indentity of the anonymous classmate he's fallen for online. Resolving both issues will be a hilarious, scary, life-changing adventure.

Duration: 1h 52m

Release Date: 2018

Genre: Romance, Romantic Comedy, Drama, Coming of Age, Comedy

Rating: 13+

Director: Greg Berlanti

Starring

Simon Spier

He's the protagonist, Simon Spier (NIck Robinson) is a relatable, kind-hearted high school teenager who grapples with coming to terms with his sexuality. Throughout Love, Simon, he navigates the challenges of being closeted, dealing with family and friends, and the fear of being outed. He’s witty, introspective, and longs for love and acceptance, all while communicating anonymously with another closeted student online.

Leah Burke

Leah Burke (Katherine Langford), Simon Spier’s best friend in Love, Simon, is loyal, caring, and emotionally supportive. She’s known Simon since childhood and senses when something is bothering him, though she struggles with her own unspoken feelings for him. Leah is intelligent, sarcastic, and values deep connections with her friends. Her journey is also one of self-discovery, as she navigates friendship, unrequited love, and eventually comes to terms with her emotions.

Emily Spier

Emily Spier (Jennifer Garner), Simon’s mother in Love, Simon, is loving, warm, and supportive. She works as a therapist and values open communication, which reflects in her strong bond with Simon. Although she initially doesn’t know about Simon’s sexuality, once he comes out, she offers unconditional love and reassurance. Emily represents a safe space for Simon, always striving to understand him and ensure that he feels accepted and loved for who he truly is.

Martin Addison

Martin Addison (Logan Miller), the antagonist in Love, Simon, is an awkward and socially clueless classmate of Simon. Though not malicious at heart, Martin’s actions cause significant turmoil. He accidentally discovers Simon’s secret and uses it to blackmail him, hoping to win the affections of another classmate. Martin is driven by insecurity and a desire to fit in, but his poor decisions lead to unintended hurt. Ultimately, he shows remorse for his actions, realizing the damage caused by his need for approval and validation.

Bram Greenfeld

Bram Greenfeld (Keiynan Lonsdale) is one of Simon Spier’s classmates and the love interest in Love, Simon. Quiet, sweet, and a bit shy, Bram is also closeted and struggles with his own journey of self-acceptance. He communicates anonymously with Simon online under the pseudonym “Blue,” forming a deep emotional connection. Despite his reserved nature, Bram is thoughtful and caring, and when he and Simon finally reveal their identities to each other, he is genuine and kind, making for a heartfelt and joyful romantic connection.

Jack Spier

Jack Spier (Josh Duhamel) is Simon’s father in Love, Simon. He’s a lighthearted and playful dad, often making jokes and maintaining a close relationship with his family. Though he sometimes unknowingly makes offhand comments that reflect societal norms about heterosexuality, he deeply loves Simon. After Simon comes out, Jack feels remorseful for not recognizing the struggles his son was facing, but he shows his love and support by apologizing and reaffirming his commitment to accepting Simon for who he is. Jack represents a loving but sometimes oblivious parent who learns and grows alongside his son.

Abby Suso

Abby Suso (Alexandra Shipp) is a friend of Simon Spier in Love, Simon. She’s vibrant, confident, and compassionate. Abby is a new addition to Simon’s friend group and quickly becomes an important and supportive presence in his life. She’s friendly and open-hearted, often serving as a source of positivity and encouragement for Simon and his friends. Throughout the film, Abby is depicted as someone who values genuine friendships and is always there to offer her support, helping to navigate the ups and downs of high school life.

Nick Eisner

Nick Eisner (Jorge Lendeborg Jr.) is a supporting character in Love, Simon. He’s one of Simon Spier’s classmates and friends. Nick is portrayed as friendly and part of Simon’s social circle. He is laid-back and enjoys hanging out with friends, contributing to the overall dynamic of the group. Although he isn’t a central figure in Simon’s journey, Nick plays a role in the broader high school experience and provides a sense of normalcy and camaraderie within the friend group.
